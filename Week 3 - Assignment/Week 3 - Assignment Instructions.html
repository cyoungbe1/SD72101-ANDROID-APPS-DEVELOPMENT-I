Week 3 ASSIGNMENT

Design a major screen component for an Android app named TimeTravelInc.   The desire to search the legacy of our predecessors has fueled the human imagination for centuries.  Vestiges of today’s art, architecture, mathematics, astronomy, even the gods we worship remain as laurels to their great triumph.  And now it is your assignment to build an Android app that helps today’s explorers to discover our origins.  Your app will enable them to discover the great mysteries of the past by visiting their greatest sanctuaries, notably, Seven Wonders of the World.  In essence, the user of this app is looking to experience time travel backwards in time, and Time Travel, Inc works to make the user travel experience so magical and awe-inspiring that he or she feels it is so.    The app should render attractively in portrait orientation on smartphone devices.  When completely coded after built upon in a future assignment, this app will consist of three screens, and one very simple introductory screen.  The purpose of this assignment is to build the most UI intensive of the screens, as shown below.  

 

The spinner element is to be loaded with entries from a string-array representing the “time travel” seven wonders destinations.  The actual values contained in the string array can be downloaded for use and consist of seven strings:   "Great Wall of China”, “Colosseum in Rome", "Machu Picchu in Peru", "Chechen Itza in Mexico", "Taj Mahal in India", "Christ the Redeemer in Brazil", and "Pyramids of Giza". 

There are two modes of travel represented as check boxes labeled “Air”, and “Air and Water”.  Notice that the checkboxes have been assigned a color.

A radio button group is used to determine the duration of the trip and the options are “4 days”, “1 week”, “2 weeks” or “1 month”.  Notice that the clickable radio buttons have been assigned a color.

A second radio button group is used to determine the type of travel package and will have implications for determining the cost of the trip in a future assignment.   The three travel package options are “Standard”, “Deluxe” or “First Class”.

              There are two clickable/pressable buttons.  The first is labeled “More Details” and is to appear adjacent to the spinner.  The button will acquire functionality in a later assignment.  The second button is an image button with text “Book my trip!”, and is to appear centered in the screen below the other UI elements.  Its app operation will be determined in a later assignment as well, but for now, when the user presses this button, the app should display all of the user selected inputs in a TextView that you are to add  temporarily for this purpose.

              The following resources have been made available to you:

1.  The screen image:  appimg.png Download appimg.png
2.  A drawable for the image button:  booktripbtn.png Download booktripbtn.png
3.  The string array of values to feed the spinner component:
